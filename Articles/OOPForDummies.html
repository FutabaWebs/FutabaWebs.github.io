What is OOP? 🕸🕸🎃🕸🕸

OOP, which stands for Object Oriented Programming, is just basically a way to model custom functions that you can call and use around in your code. It simplifies the complexity and time consuming need to re-write complex programming algorithms from scratch, making your life easier. A perfect example of what I mean by that would be modules like with Python, were you can just “import’ or “include” something and you just call an “object” for it and you’re good to go.

What is OOP (Members)? 🕸🕸🎃🕸🕸

You’ve probably seen me mention something about “members” that belong to a class, where you can “call them” and use any that are available to you. Members are basically the overall functions that make up classes and can even be made available to other classes that need them via “Polymorphism”, which is a fancy term for “inheritance”, which is where other classes you’ve written “inherit” functions belonging to other classes you have Think of it like “biology”, where you “inherit’ dominant and recessive traits stemming from your parents.
